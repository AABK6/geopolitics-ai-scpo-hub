## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are Sciences Po students and instructors navigating a course on the geopolitics of AI. Students need to quickly grasp the seminar's central thesis, move through the eight modules, and understand that their final research projects are part of the course architecture rather than peripheral add-ons.

### Brand Personality
Academic, ambitious, and inventive. The interface should feel intellectually serious and editorially credible, while also demonstrating that AI-assisted making can expand creative possibility. The emotional goal is not "tech hype"; it is curiosity, authority, and a sense of entering a living research environment.

### Aesthetic Direction
Thesis first, then spectacle. The landing experience should foreground the course argument within five seconds, while the 3D hub functions as a beautiful curricular inference network rather than a generic sci-fi artifact. The visual language should avoid trend-chasing AI motifs and instead combine architectural atmosphere, editorial typography, and polished spatial rendering. Student projects should appear as integral cohort research nodes. Empty projects should be visibly in-progress, not falsely complete.

### Design Principles
1. Put the thesis before the effect.
2. Use 3D to explain the curriculum, not to obscure it.
3. Treat student research nodes as integral to the course structure.
4. Keep the tone academically credible, not startup-cyberpunk.
5. Make incompleteness legible and intentional during early weeks of the course.
